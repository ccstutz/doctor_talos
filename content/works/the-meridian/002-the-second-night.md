On the third night it came back.

Longer this time — seven minutes, forty seconds — and with something inside it that she could not account for. A pattern that repeated every ninety-one seconds. She checked the equipment twice and then sat very still.

Ninety-one is a prime number. She was not sure if that mattered. She wrote it down anyway, in the margin, and circled it.

She had been doing this work for eleven years and had learned that the things you circle in the margin are almost never the things that end up mattering, but you circle them anyway because you don't know yet, and not knowing is the whole job.
