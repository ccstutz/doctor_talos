The signal came in on a frequency no one was monitoring. That is not unusual. Most things arrive that way.

She wrote down the time, the bearing, the duration. Three minutes and eleven seconds of something that was not quite noise. She had learned to distinguish the categories: noise, signal, and the third thing, which had no name yet and which she suspected was the most important of the three.

She filed the log and went to make coffee. By the time she returned, the signal had stopped. This was also not unusual. She sat down and waited for it to come back.

It didn't. Not that night.
